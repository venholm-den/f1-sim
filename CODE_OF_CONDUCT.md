# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

This project should be a respectful, practical place to discuss and improve the F1 simulation tooling. Everyone participating in issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and related project spaces is expected to act with care and professionalism.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but be clear when something needs to change.
- Keep feedback focused on the project, code, documentation, data, and decisions.
- Welcome people with different levels of Formula 1, Python, modelling, and open-source experience.
- Respect privacy and do not share private information without permission.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Trolling, repeated disruption, or bad-faith argument.
- Publishing private information, credentials, tokens, or private contact details.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe or unwelcome.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or block participants when behavior violates this code of conduct.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the project maintainer through the repository owner account. Reports will be handled with as much confidentiality as practical.

## Scope

This code applies in project spaces and in public spaces when someone is representing the project or its community.
